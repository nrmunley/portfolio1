Fruition Brewing’s Challenge

Fruition Brewing is a young brewery offering beers that focus on quality and balance. They just signed a lease on a new taproom which will be opening in early 2019 and they needed a website that matches their brand and the quality of their products.

Research

I approached this problem from two sides. First, I wanted to understand what users thought of Fruition’s current site and secondly, I wanted to understand what beer brands people like and how much they understand about the brewing process.

Surveys - Potential customers

The goal of this second survey was to get a pulse on Fruition’s potential customers. What beer brands they like and why, what beer styles they are into, and how much do they know about the beer brewing process. Logo, layout, and ease of use seemed like the most important aspects of beer websites that people liked.

Competitive Analysis

Who is the competition? How can Fruition differentiate themselves? How can they make their brand more appealing than other breweries through the content on their website? In all their competitors I didn’t feel like they did a good job of telling their story, why they started brewing beer, how they started brewing and the techniques and ingredients they use.

Weaknesses Opportunities
  • Not a clear story behind the beer
  • Little mention about the brewing process and philosophy behind the beers being made
  • Little mention about the ingredients used in their beers and how they source them
  • Tell Fruition’s story better than competitors
  • Inform consumers about the philosophy behind the beer brewing process at Fruition including the sourcing local ingredients

User Personas

Using the results from the surveys I conducted, coupled with the documentation on demographics and target market provided by the owners of Fruition I developed user personas to help guide the design process.

Michael H.

Casual | Daily Consumer

43 Yo | Organic Farmer

Motivations: After working long hours in his fields and managing a small team of farmers Michael enjoys to blow off some steam and relieve the days stress before heading home. He doesn't care what Fruition is pouring or what food is available. He just likes a relaxing place to wash the stresses from the day away before heading home to his family.

Goals: Stress relief, forgetting about the hard day's work and getting his head right before seeing his wife and kids.

Frustrations: No convenient place in the neighborhood to stop on the way home from work especially not one with the relaxing vibe he is looking for. No breweries near his home that support the local farmers and the local ecosystem.

Jennifer T.

Casual | Commuter

22 Yo | Student

Motivations: Jennifer takes classes at Cabrillo College a couple times a week. It is about a 45 min drive home from class on a good day with no traffic. She likes to split her drive up by stopping at Fruition particularly after a stressful test day.

Goals: Stress relief, celebrating hard work and taking a break from the busy roads.

Frustrations: There is no convenient place on her route home with a good environment that is welcoming and relaxing at the same time.

User Flows

From the user stories, I developed user flows to help visualize the user's interactions within the site.

Card Sorting

I did a card sorting exercise to better understand how potential users would group various sections on the site and make sure sections of the site were where people expected them to be.

Sitemap

Using the results from the card sorting exercise, the flows developed based on my research and meetings with the client I landed on the following site map. The green highlighted area represents what will be on the site today, and the red area shows the additional pages and sections that will be on the site as the business matures.

Wireframes & Mockups

After doing my initial research I started to explore layouts through a process of iteration; moving from sketches to mockups and refining based on critiques and testing.

Sketches

I started to layout my ideas on paper to help understand how all the elements might flow together.

LoFi Mockups

I moved a couple of my ideas into LoFi mockups, bringing several options to the clients to get a better sense of what they were envisioning.

HiFi Landing

I iterated on the work done on the LoFi mockups and using the color palette provided by the branding agency the client previously worked with came up with three different options for the landing page to present to the client. Both the clients and I thought that version 2 was by far the best use of the color palette.

HiFi Layouts

After getting feedback from the client I built out the rest of the layouts for each of the pages of the site in order to do some early user testing.

User Testing

I tested the HiFi mockups to ensure that the information was organized in an intuitive manner and that the flow of the site worked.

Usability

All participants were able to complete the four (4) tasks with a 100% completion rate:

  1. Find the details about Fruition’s Pale Ale.
  2. Find the current food menu.
  3. Locate information about David Purgason the head brewer.
  4. Locate the companies email address.

Design Revisions

After compiling the results from testing and going through several design critiques I made some much-needed revisions to the design.

Added an indicator icon so users can more easily see where they are in the site.
I decreased the size of the body text and increased the line height to increase readability.
I decreased the size of the leaf icon page breaks and Changed the sizing and tracking on my headers.
I updated the beer labels which will be consistent with the hover labels on the home screen and made various other small changes.

Logo Color Preference Testing

I wanted to see if I could add some color to the logo on the landing, but I didn’t want to make assumptions. After preference testing the following versions, neither design won out so I went with what the clients preferred highlighted below in green.

Form vs. Button Link Preference Testing

I then tested whether users preferred an email button which opened their default email or whether they preferred a Form in the ‘Contact Us’ section of the site. Once again opinions were split so I made an executive decision to go with a button link which looks cleaner and has a more modern feel.

Timeline Infographic Preference Testing

The last thing I tested was the timeline infographic in the ‘About Us’ section of the site with the goal of visually showing Fruitions history. I tested the three options shown and the Tree infographic (version 2) won out easily, but after review, I decided to leave it out entirely because Fruition is still such a young brewery and the information displayed didn't add to the design or experience of the website.

What I learned

This project was a great exercise in client communication and the importance of sticking to my design decisions and helping clients understand these decisions. At times I struggled to get information that I needed from the client to keep the project moving forward and it helped me realize that people have different communication styles.

The branding for this project was done by another company which helped me learn how to apply someone else's branding guidelines where applicable.

This project also reinforced that sometimes less is more. A lot of features of the site were left out partly because of the maturity of this business, but also because they didn't really add to the user's experience.
