About

I first learned of UX design roughly 6 years ago when I was working on the self-driving car project at Google[X], where I was able to play an integral role in a team that led a study on behalf of Google at the Virginia Tech Transportation Institute. As consultants to the University, we helped set up the testing environment, wrote a user testing script and administered the tests. The experience of both administering and participating in user studies that helped shape design decisions affecting how a self-driving car operator interacts with a self-driving car really led me to start thinking about a career in UX design.

The problem-solving skills I learned studying Civil Engineering in college coupled with a creative youth filled with ceramics and architectural design courses have helped me find a balance between data-driven functionality and aesthetically pleasing solutions.

Through my coursework at Bloc and working on several design projects I have been able to learn and apply design principles which create consistent products and experiences with an emphasis on usability.
