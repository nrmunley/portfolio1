Newlocal's Story

It is often hard to find good places to watch English Premier League soccer matches in the USA. The games are on extremely early in the morning and if you don’t have specific television channels then you can’t view the games. There are bars that open early in order for fans to gather and watch the games, but they are often hard to find. This application will help fans find bars that will be televising the game in cities they are unfamiliar with, very much like a finely tailored version of Yelp.

Research

Initial research was invaluable when it came to beginning the process of defining the projects MVP; learning which location-based applications people use as well as how they use them. Leveraging user surveys and competitive analysis I was able to steer the project in the right direction at an early stage.

Surveys

The goal of the surveys I ran was to understand how people currently use location-based search applications, what the most common location-based search applications are and to find out about location-based search applications that I was unfamiliar with. As well as, the positives and negatives of each. I found that 75% of users tested used Yelp to locate bars and restaurants, but when asked specifically about sports bars there was no common application used. The only trend was that 35% of people surveyed defaulted to Google search when looking for sports bars.

Competitive Analysis

Weaknesses Opportunities
- Old out-dated branding that doesn’t speak to the modern consumer - Can have additional features that competitors don’t offer
- Too much content not targeted in the correct way. - Be more specific with what our users are searching for and target users and business' based on demographics

I did a competitive analysis of three different applications to establish who the potential competition might be and how newlocal could differentiate itself. I looked at the Yelp application, Bar Finder application and EPL Live application and found several weaknesses of these companies leaving ample opportunity to jump into the market.
